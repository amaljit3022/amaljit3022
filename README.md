## About

I work at the intersection of civil engineering and software. My background is in public water
infrastructure, and my development work focuses on turning engineering workflows into tools that
are reproducible, testable, and easier to review.

My main areas of interest are:

- **Hydrology and hydraulics** — transparent models, traceable inputs, and reproducible results.
- **Geospatial engineering** — GIS, remote sensing, maps, and spatial data workflows.
- **Engineering computation** — units, geometry, physics, validation, and tested calculations.
- **Responsible automation** — local-first AI workflows with explicit scope and limitations.

Unless a repository explicitly states otherwise, these are independent projects and are not
operational forecasts, signed engineering designs, or official departmental systems.

## How I build

- Start with a clear problem, user, scope, and non-goals.
- Keep source data and assumptions traceable.
- Prefer small, testable components over opaque calculations.
- Document installation, verification, limitations, and project status honestly.
- Treat privacy, licensing, and reproducibility as engineering requirements.

## Current direction

- Reproducible tools for water and environmental engineering.
- Geospatial data platforms for analysis and decision support.
- Better testing, CI, documentation, and provenance for engineering software.
- Local-first AI workflows that keep users in control of their data.
